1

Open Advanced Search

Getting in
Where: Log in to Zoominfo → look for Advanced Search. You will always start here.

Make sure you're on the Companies tab (top of the filter panel — you can flip between Companies and Contacts). We're building the company list first.

2

Location — enter your assigned geography, set to Company HQ

Companies tab
Where: Type location in the search filters box → click anywhere in the gray dropdown box → you'll land on State or City.

Enter each of your assigned states, pressing Enter after each one. Check with your VP if you're not sure of your list. You only do this once (see Step 3).

❗ Very important — do not skip
  • Find the Location of dropdown → set it to Company HQ.
  • You can call contacts who live anywhere — but the company's HQ must be in your territory.
  • This one setting keeps your whole list in-territory.
3

Save it as your Starter Template

Do once, reuse forever
Where: Top right → Save and Subscribe

Name it something like:

Starter Template - States
❗ Do NOT turn on notifications for this one...

It will drive you insane if you do. Just save.

  1. Click Save.
  2. From now on: top right → Open Saved Searches → click your template.
  3. Star it so it's always at the top. You never re-enter your states again.
4

Employee count — hunt deer, not elephants

Companies tab
Where: Type employee count in the search filters box.
Min
51
Max
1000
🏃 Rabbits, deer, elephants
  • You have no ICP floor — every firm size is yours.
  • Deer (ICP 3, 51–1,000 staff): real budget, reachable decision makers — start here.
  • Small firms (≤50) close in a call or two; enterprise (1,001+) runs 60–180 days.
  • Work both ends — but feed your family in the middle.
⚡ Platform priority rule — non-negotiable
  • 1. Always lead with Lattira Spec.
  • 2. ConstructionWire only when BD pain surfaces naturally.
  • 3. QuestCDN only if the firm manages public bid document distribution.
  • Expect "who else is using this?" — answer with the NBS heritage (98%+ of top UK architects) and early-adopter positioning.
🚫 Skip the revenue filter entirely
  • Public revenue data is garbage.
  • I've seen a $20M company listed at $900K — and a $2M company listed at $33M.
  • Employee count is your size filter. Period.

Industries — paste NAICS codes in bulk

Companies tab
Where: Type naics in the search filters box → click anywhere in the box → click Add Codes in Bulk → paste → Apply.

Why NAICS instead of the industry filter?

  • "Architecture & Planning" in the industry search is like searching the word soda — a ton of noise.
  • NAICS codes are government classifications: if industry is "red," a NAICS code is "maroon."

Contact info + kill the assistants
Contacts tab

    
  

  

    
Where: Type contact info in the search filters box → check Mobile Phone and Business Email.

    
This is where the list really concentrates — only people you can actually reach.

    
Then: Type job title again → type assistant → click Exclude.

    
Executives' assistants show up under executive-sounding titles. Cut them.

    
📊 Sanity check: the 411-contacts-at-3-companies problem

    
    
      
  • Flip back to the Companies tab and compare the two counts.
    • 
      
  • You want roughly 2 contacts per company.
    • 
      
  • 411 contacts at 3 companies = your company filters need work. Go back before you export anything.
